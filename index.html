🌡️ About this Application

As cities grow, concrete and asphalt absorb and re-emit solar radiation, creating pockets of intense heat — the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect. In Melbourne, surface temperatures vary by up to 10°C across just a few kilometres.

PURPOSE
The Urban Heat Explorer maps Land Surface Temperature across Melbourne and examines how building typology and tree canopy interact with surface heat patterns.
AIM
To provide an interactive, browser-based tool for exploring the spatial relationship between urban heat, building roof type, and tree canopy coverage in Melbourne.
